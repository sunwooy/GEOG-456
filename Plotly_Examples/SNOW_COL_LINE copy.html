I used a line chart for Lake Mead Water elevation and for the Willow Park Snow Water Equivalent. A benefit of using a line chart is it becomes easier to track the order of rise in both water elevation and snow water equivalent. When I left it as just a scattergram, there were just too many points that looking for patterns became hard. By making this adjustment, peaks in snow water equivalent was also easy to find as you track through time. However, a limitation is that you can't show statistical significance (similar to a scattergram).
